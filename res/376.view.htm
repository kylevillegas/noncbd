1468<h3>

1469Facts on tennis injuries</h3>

1470

1471<p>

1472Tennis is a popular international sport catering to all ages and skill levels.Statistics from the Australian Sports Commissions 2006 survey showed an estimated 1,130,700 Australians aged 15 years and older played tennis in the 12 months prior to being surveyed.Tennis is a sport that can be played on a variety of surfaces (grass, artificial grass, hard court surfaces such as plexicushion and plexipave and clay/en- tout-cas), which requires speed, power, endurance, balance and coordination. As a result, injuries can and do occur.</p>

1473

1474<p>

1475<strong>

1476How many injuries?</strong>

1477</p>

1478

1479<ul>

1480

1481<li>

1482The rate of tennis injury in the general population is five injuries per 1,000 hours of participation.</li>

1483

1484<li>

1485From 2002-2003, 505 people were admitted to hospitals across Australia for tennis-related injuries, at a rate of 33 injuries per 100,000 tennis players.</li>

1486

1487<li>

1488In 2006, 127 people were admitted to Victorian hospitals while 382 people visited Victorian emergency departments for tennis-related injuries.</li>

1489

1490</ul>

1491

1492<p>

1493<strong>

1494The causes and types of injuries</strong>

1495</p>

1496

1497<ul>

1498

1499<li>

1500Lower limb (ankle, knee, and thigh) injuries are most common and are caused by the sprinting, stopping, pivoting and pounding nature of tennis. Lower limb tennis injuries are acute (e.g. ankle sprain) or chronic (e.g. knee tendon pain).</li>

1501

1502<li>

1503Upper limb (elbow, shoulder, wrist) injuries are usually caused by the high-velocity and repetitive arm movements required in tennis. These injuries tendto be overuse in nature (e.g. tennis elbow).</li>

1504

1505<li>

1506Back injuries and pain are common due to the rotation required to hit groundstrokes, and the combinationof rotation, extension and lateral flexion involved inthe serve.</li>

1507

1508</ul>

1509

1510<p>

1511<strong>

1512Factors affecting your injury risk</strong>

1513</p>

1514

1515<ul>

1516

1517<li>

1518Different court surfaces.</li>

1519

1520<li>

1521Condition of tennis balls used.</li>

1522

1523<li>

1524Type of racquet.</li>

1525

1526<li>

1527Playing technique.</li>

1528

1529<li>

1530Weather extremes.</li>

1531

1532<li>

1533Inappropriate footwear.</li>

1534

1535<li>

1536Poor physical conditioning.</li>

1537

1538<li>

1539The amount and level of participation.</li>

1540

1541<li>

1542Poor injury rehabilitation.</li>

1543

1544</ul>

1545

1546<p>

1547<strong>

1548Safety tips for tennis</strong>

1549</p>

1550

1551<ul>

1552

1553<li>

1554Good preparation is important</li>

1555

1556<li>

1557Avoid playing with a pre-existing illness or injury. If in doubt, talk to a medical practitioner.</li>

1558

1559<li>

1560Always warm up, stretch and cool down.</li>

1561

1562<li>

1563Maintain an adequate fitness level. Undertake conditioning and training exercises specific to the physical demands of tennis.</li>

1564

1565</ul>

1566

1567<p>

1568<strong>

1569Good technique and practices will help prevent injury</strong>

1570</p>

1571

1572<ul>

1573

1574<li>

1575Seek instruction from a Tennis Australia qualified coach to develop correct skills and techniques.</li>

1576

1577<li>

1578Avoid over-repetition of any one type of shot. Practise a range of strokes including groundstrokes, serves, return of serves, overhead smashes and volleys.</li>

1579

1580</ul>

1581

1582<p>

1583<strong>

1584Use appropriate equipment and make the environment safe</strong>

1585</p>

1586

1587<ul>

1588

1589<li>

1590Use a racquet suitable for your style of play and physical capabilities. Players, especially those with arm and shoulder injuries, should seek professional advice when selecting a racquet and choosing string tension.</li>

1591

1592<li>

1593Use tennis balls appropriate for the playing surface. Avoid using wet or flat/dead balls.</li>

1594

1595<li>

1596Check and maintain the playing surface to ensure it is in good condition and free of hazards.</li>

1597

1598<li>

1599Wear the right protective equipment</li>

1600

1601<li>

1602Seek professional advice on footwear.</li>

1603

1604<li>

1605Players with a history of joint injury should seek professional advice about taping or bracing before play.</li>

1606

1607</ul>

1608

1609<p>

1610<strong>

1611Modify rules and equipment for children</strong>

1612</p>

1613

1614<ul>

1615

1616<li>

1617Encourage children and beginners to participate in grassroots tennis programs such as Aviva Tennis Hot Shots or similar beginner programs delivered by local clubs and coaches, to introduce new players to the game through modified equipment such as mini-nets and decompression balls. This will help new players develop good tennis skills and correct technique.</li>

1618

1619<li>

1620Children should use equipment suitable to their age, size and skill level.</li>

1621

1622</ul>

1623

1624<p>

1625<strong>

1626Other safety tips</strong>

1627</p>

1628

1629<p>

1630&nbsp;</p>

1631

1632<ul>

1633

1634<li>

1635<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">

1636Be sunsmart. Wear sun protective clothing, a hat, sunglasses and SPF 30+ sunscreen.</span>

1637</li>

1638

1639<li>

1640<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">

1641In hot conditions, seek shade before, during and after play, and avoid playing in the middle of the day, if possible, when UV rays are most intense.</span>

1642</li>

1643

1644<li>

1645<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">

1646Eat a well-balanced diet.</span>

1647</li>

1648

1649<li>

1650<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">

1651Drink water before, during and after play.</span>

1652</li>

1653

1654<li>

1655<span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">

1656Exercise caution when playing in extreme heat/humidity or wet/cold conditions.</span>

1657</li>

1658

1659</ul>

1660

1661<p>

1662<strong>

1663If an injury occurs</strong>

1664</p>

1665

1666<p>

1667&nbsp;</p>

1668

1669<ul>

1670

1671<li>

1672Stop playing if you experience an injury or illness.</li>

1673

1674<li>

1675Injured players should seek prompt attention from qualified first aid personnel or a sports medicine professional.</li>

1676

1677<li>

1678Injuries should be fully rehabilitated before returning to play.</li>

1679

1680</ul>
